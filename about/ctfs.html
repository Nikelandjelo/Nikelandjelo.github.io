Capture The Flag (CTF)

One of the best methods of learning and practising cybersecurity for me are CTFs.

Photo by Tima Miroshnichenko from Pexels

What are CTFs for me?

As an ex-gamer, I can say that the CTFs for me are like games. The friendships formed and the idea of teamwork give me a sense of belonging somewhere that every game can. Moreover, they are giving me the opportunity of learning and practising hacking and even practising report writing.

My experience with CTFs

UTCTF (2021)

Ben Roxbee Cox and I came 129th on UTCTF (weight of almost 38 on CTFtime), out of almost 700 teams! This is my first CTF, and it is so inspiring that two first-year university students went so far. For more info, you can check out Ben's writeups on the CTF.

On the list below are the platforms I have any experience with. This experience includes mounts and years to just hours or minutes.

  • TryHackMe
  • Hack The Box
  • PortSwigger
  • VulnHub
  • OverTheWire (Wargames)
  • BlueTimeCTF

HTB

Hack The Box is so far one of the best places for practising hacking in my experience. Without any doubt, I can practice skills that I have already developed and new ones that I will need further in my career.
